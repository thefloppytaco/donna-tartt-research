# Donna Tartt — Primary-Source Interview Index

Scope: interviews, Q&As, on-stage conversations, and radio/TV/podcast appearances **in which Donna Tartt herself speaks**. Excludes critical reviews, encyclopedia entries, and articles about Tartt that do not quote her directly. A separate Section P tracks Tartt's own bylines (also primary-source in her voice, but not interviews).

Type abbreviations: `INT` = interview · `Q&A` · `CONV` = on-stage / book-event conversation · `TV` · `RADIO` · `POD` = podcast.

Counts: Print/online interviews — 56 · Television appearances — 6 · Radio/podcast appearances — 8 · Public events — 4 · Tartt's own bylines — 15. Full primary-source text for 15 interviews is captured to `reference/interviews/originals/` (see Section 0).

## Notes on scope

Per the primary-sources-only filter, reference/encyclopedic pages (Britannica, Mississippi Encyclopedia, Penguin author pages, Pulitzer, etc.) and review/criticism pieces (Kakutani, James Wood, Evgenia Peretz's "It's Tartt — But Is It Art?", First Things on her Catholicism, Bradshaw's Goldfinch film review, etc.) are excluded from this index, since they are not Tartt speaking in her own voice.

Borderline items excluded for the same reason:
- The Dazed Digital "Your guide to mysterious literary genius Donna Tartt" — composite secondary profile, no new Tartt quotes.
- Esquire 2019 Lili Anolik "Money, Madness, Cocaine and Literary Genius" — Tartt declined to be interviewed; an oral history of others discussing her cohort.
- The 2021 *Once Upon a Time at Bennington College* podcast — Tartt is the absent subject, not a speaker.
- The Diane Rehm 2014 "Readers' Review" — panel discussion without Tartt's voice.
- Critic-profiles that quote Tartt only from prior interviews (no new sit-down).
