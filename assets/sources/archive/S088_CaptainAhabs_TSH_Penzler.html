THE SECRET HISTORY - INSCRIBED TO OTTO PENZLER

Author: Tartt, Donna

Publisher: New York: Alfred A. Knopf, 1992. First Edition.

Item #7445

Price: $1,850.00

First Printing. Octavo (24.5cm); original pictorial boards; publisher's printed acetate dustjacket; [xiv],[8]-523,[7]pp. Inscribed by the author on the upper margin of the title page, a week prior to publication, to bibliophile and publisher Otto Penzler and his wife: “To Carolyn + Otto / Warm regards / Donna Tartt / 9/10/92.” Light production wrinkle to upper board corners, else Fine in a Near Fine dustjacket, unclipped (priced $23.00), with some of the usual light surface wear.

The Pulitzer Prize-winning author’s first book, an inverted detective story centered around a group of six students studying the classics at Hampden College in Vermont. This copy with a pre-publication inscription to noted bibliophile and publisher Otto Penzler; while Penzler never published Tartt’s work, he did convince her to contribute to a poem (“True Crime”) to Murder For Love, a 1996 anthology of short stories he edited for Delacorte Press. An attractive copy of a novel that is almost single-handedly credited for popularizing the “dark academia” genre.
