# Verification & Corrections

This document records how the factual claims in the Donna Tartt collectors reference were checked, what errors were found, and what they were corrected to. It is intended as a standalone account of the verification methodology and the durable bibliographic corrections that resulted.

**Method:** Every factual claim was cross-referenced against primary and secondary sources, including publisher pages, American Library Association (ALA) press releases, award databases, encyclopedia infoboxes, contemporary press, and archived HTML source files. Follow-up rounds added cross-referencing against marketplace listings, publisher product pages (Scala, Folio Society, Rizzoli, Diogenes, University of Georgia Press, Wayne State University Press), the Deutsche Nationalbibliothek (DNB) catalog, Open Library, and Internet Archive metadata.

A source-confidence scheme was applied throughout, distinguishing claims directly confirmed by a primary source from those corroborated by multiple independent secondary sources, those resting on a single credible source, and those grounded only in collector tradition without documentary support.

## Confirmed Errors and Corrections Applied

### Error 1 — *The Little Friend* UK/US publication dates described as "simultaneous"

**What the document said:** that *The Little Friend* had a "simultaneous October 28, 2002 UK/US launch."

**What the sources show:**
- US Knopf publication date: **October 22, 2002** — confirmed by the encyclopedia infobox for *The Little Friend*, the Penguin Random House publisher page (ISBN 9780375704566), and a contemporary review referring to the novel arriving October 22.
- UK Bloomsbury publication date: **October 28, 2002** — confirmed by a primary trade-press interview, a publisher embargo memo, and the reference's own Deluxe Limited Edition bibliographic entry.
- The two dates are **six days apart**; the publications were not simultaneous.

**Correction:** references to a "simultaneous" launch were replaced with the October 28, 2002 UK launch occurring six days after the October 22, 2002 US Knopf publication.

---

### Error 2 — *The Goldfinch* page count: encyclopedia attribution wrong

**What the document said:** "Pages: 771 (per fedpo); 784 (Wikipedia / publisher catalogue)."

**What the sources show:**
- The encyclopedia infobox for *The Goldfinch* gives **771 pages**, not 784.
- The Hachette Book Group publisher catalogue (ISBN 9780316055437) gives **784 pages**.
- The "784" figure belongs to the publisher catalogue only; the encyclopedia had been incorrectly grouped with it.

**Correction:** "771 (per fedpo and encyclopedia infobox); 784 (per Hachette publisher catalogue)."

---

### Error 3 — Andrew Carnegie Medal date stated as "January 26, 2014"

**What the document said:** that Tartt won the Andrew Carnegie Medal for Fiction on January 26, 2014, citing an ALA URL that returns a 404.

**What the sources show:**
- An ALA press release dated July 1, 2014 states the selections were announced on **June 28** at the Andrew Carnegie Medals for Excellence in Fiction and Nonfiction Awards Presentation, held during the ALA Annual Conference in Las Vegas.
- January 26, 2014 was ALA Midwinter in Philadelphia; the Andrew Carnegie Medal is **not** announced at Midwinter — only lists such as the Reading List and Notable Books are.
- The correct, active ALA press release URL is `https://www.ala.org/news/2014/07/goldfinch-bully-pulpit-win-2014-andrew-carnegie-medals-excellence-fiction-and`.

**Correction:** the date was corrected to June 28, 2014, at the ALA Annual Conference in Las Vegas; the broken citation URL was replaced with the verified ALA press release, and its confidence label was upgraded to reflect an active primary source.

---

### Error 4 — WH Smith Literary Award 1993 for *The Secret History* (Tartt did not win)

**What the document said:** that Tartt won the WH Smith Literary Award for *The Secret History* in 1993 as well as for *The Little Friend* in 2003, and was "one of few authors to win this award twice."

**What the sources show:**
- The WH Smith Literary Award winner list records the **1993 winner as Michèle Roberts, *Daughters of the House***.
- Tartt's awards record lists only the **2003 WH Smith Literary Award for *The Little Friend*** — no 1993 award.
- No source confirms a 1993 WH Smith win for Tartt or *The Secret History*. The error likely arose from confusion between a shortlisting or longlisting and an actual win.

**Correction:** the 1993 award row was deleted; the "won twice" claim was removed; the narrative was corrected to state Tartt won the WH Smith Literary Award once, for *The Little Friend* (2003); and the footnote was rewritten to note that the 1993 winner was Michèle Roberts.

## Minor Annotations (informational, not errors)

**Same piece under two titles.** "Basketball Season" (Oxford American #2, Fall 1992) and "Team Spirit" (Harper's vol. 288, April 1994) are the same underlying autobiographical piece published under two different titles, the Oxford American version predating the Harper's version by roughly 18 months. Listing them as separate items is bibliographically accurate (two publication events) but could mislead readers into thinking they are different texts. No correction to factual content is required.

**"The Ambush" — dual publication venue.** The reference attributes "The Ambush" solely to The Guardian (June 25, 2005). The BASS 2006 article lists the story's source as *Tin House* magazine; the story likely appeared in both. The Guardian attribution is not wrong, but Tin House should be noted as an additional (and BASS-canonical) source.

## Open Items

Some items could not be definitively resolved during verification and remain flagged for copy-in-hand examination or deeper archival research.

### Baseline open items

1. **The Secret History trial binding.** A single ABA/PBFA dealer description is the sole source for the "trial binding" designation and the characterization that it was "deemed unsuccessful." No independent second source confirms this; confidence remains single-source. Copy-in-hand examination is the only resolution path.

2. **The Secret History per-printing collation completeness.** The 5th, 6th, and 8th printings are confirmed to exist via dealer descriptions, but no primary-source copy-in-hand collation of their specific identifier points has been completed. Book-of-the-Month Club and Quality Paperback Club edition identification also remains secondary-source only.

3. **Tartt inscription ink palette.** The brown / blue / copper / purple / Venetian ink palette used for authentication is documented in collector tradition, but high-resolution cross-reference images have not been assembled. This is a reference-utility gap rather than a factual error.

### Contributions and forewords — resolved

A round of full-text examination of contributor volumes closed a number of open questions:

- **NGA Scala, *A World of Art* (1999).** Full examination of two copies returned zero matches for "Tartt"; the title page credits Martha Richler as sole author, and the introduction and author note name Richler only. The marketplace co-credit was a metadata-aggregator artifact. Reclassified as a **misidentification** — Tartt does not appear in this volume.

- **The Same River Twice (2014).** Full examination returned zero matches for "Tartt" across the volume; the editor is Connie Culpepper. The marketplace headline-author attribution is confirmed as a metadata-aggregator default error. Reclassified as a **misidentification**.

  With both of the above reclassified, the earliest confirmed Tartt foreword/introduction is the Charles Portis *True Grit* (Bloomsbury UK reissue, January 17, 2005).

- **Life of St Francis (HarperCollins Spiritual Classics, 2005).** Foreword confirmed at pp. v–ix (~1,100 words), signed "Donna Tartt" with explicit copyright. It includes an autobiographical disclosure of Tartt's Mississippi-fundamentalist childhood and her introduction to St. Francis at age fourteen via Salinger's *Franny and Zooey* — a notable primary-source datum for any biographical treatment.

- **When We Were Young (Bloomsbury, 2004).** Tartt's untitled piece confirmed at pp. 294–297 (~660 words), a childhood reminiscence of seeing her first hummingbird at age four with her great-grandmother. The contributor bio lists her residence as Virginia and New York City as of 2004.

- **Legendary Authors and the Clothes They Wore (2017).** Tartt is chapter 31 (a ~940-word style profile), correcting an earlier draft that placed her as the opening profile. Quotations are sourced to prior interviews and the NYTBR "By the Book" feature.

- **By the Book, ed. Pamela Paul (Henry Holt, 2014).** The reprinted NYTBR interview's original publication date confirmed as Sunday, October 20, 2013 — two days before the US release of *The Goldfinch*. Henry Holt page range estimated at pp. ~280–290 (reflowed digital edition strips printed pagination).

- **Fairy Tale Review Vol. 1, Blue Issue (2005).** Tartt's piece confirmed at pp. 66–71 (~1,600 words, 5 footnotes). Title disambiguation resolved: running head "On Barrie and Stevenson"; TOC heading "From Barrie to Stevenson." Notably, Tartt names Stevenson as the model for *The Little Friend*.

- **Fairy Tale Review Vol. 2, Green Issue (2006).** The four poems Tartt translated identified as "Bottom," "Being Beauteous," "Lives" (Vies), and "Phrases," all from Arthur Rimbaud's *Les Illuminations*, at pp. 128–135 in en-face layout. This is the only documented Tartt translation of another author's work.

- **Cornbread Nation 5 (University of Georgia Press, 2014).** Reprint provenance confirmed documentarily from the volume's own source-credit page: "Early Times in Mississippi," originally published in Garden and Gun, November/December 2007. Tartt's piece is at pp. 275–277.

- **Folio Society, *We Have Always Lived in the Castle* (2023).** Tartt's piece confirmed as an afterword at pp. 177–185 (~2,300 words), signed "Donna Tartt" — structurally an afterword at the back of the book, not a front-matter introduction. The colophon's "introducer" credit is the generic sense, not a position label. Production details: 500 numbered plus 20 lettered copies (520 signed total), typeset in Arno and Zachar Display, printed at Graphicom, Italy, bound in screen-printed and blocked cloth with a Hoffmeister design, in a clamshell box with one loose signed Hoffmeister print. Tartt's signature appears in black ink on the orange colophon stock, with Hoffmeister's signature below.

### Contributions — confirmed but pending detail

- **Mein Lieblingsmensch (Diogenes, 2022).** Tartt's contributor status is confirmed (named in the publisher description; full 20-contributor list confirmed from publisher metadata). The specific German title of her piece, its source text, the translator, and the page range remain unidentified pending copy-in-hand examination. A likely source-text candidate is "Schottenmütze" (= Tam-O'-Shanter), reused from the 1995 Goldmann anthology. The reason the Deluxe edition runs 336 pp versus the paperback's 256 pp is also unknown.

- **The Colors of Sies Marjan (Rizzoli, 2022).** Tartt's contributor status is confirmed; the format is narrowed to an interview or a written reflection (the publisher description lists "interviews and reflections" from a named group including Tartt). Confirmed volume detail: 400 pp, hardcover, organized by color spectrum, with a foreword by Elizabeth Peyton. The exact title, page range, length, and format of Tartt's piece remain pending copy-in-hand examination.

- **Sommer, Sonne, Sand und mehr (Goldmann, 1995).** The DNB-verified TOC confirms Tartt's "Schottenmütze" at pp. 96–101 (= Tam-O'-Shanter). The source-citations page (p. 408) would identify the translator. Companion volumes in the Goldmann *Ferien-Lesebuch* series may also include Tartt; this is unconfirmed.

- **D — La Repubblica delle donne.** This Italian weekly shows Tartt and Damon Albarn on the cover, but the issue date and number are not yet established and would require a higher-resolution view of the masthead.

### Contributions — secondary follow-ups

- **NGA Scala paperback cover and Spanish translation.** Contingent on the NGA Scala determination above (now a misidentification), the paperback cover image and the question of whether the Spanish translation (*Un mundo de arte*) would have retained any Tartt introduction are moot or unrecoverable from public sources.

- **The Same River Twice launch event.** A documented 2014 launch event included readings by contributors; whether Tartt attended is unknown and, given her established media reticence, unlikely. This is moot following the misidentification above.

### Marketplace and press attributions

- **ACTUEL N°31–32 (July–August 1993, "SEXE" issue) — confirmed positive.** Tartt **is** in this issue: a full PORTRAITS feature, "TARTT, AU POIL!" by Frédéric Taddeï, pp. 170–171, with an author portrait. The piece is a France-vs-US polemic praising *The Secret History* (*Le Maître des illusions*, Plon, 1993). An earlier negative finding had relied on a partial table-of-contents image that omitted the Portraits section; this illustrates a general verification principle — an issue should not be cleared on a table-of-contents image unless that index is verified complete, since absence from a TOC does not establish absence from the issue.

- **Andy Warhol's Interview Magazine, Vol. XXII No. 11.** Confirmed to be November 1991 (Keanu Reeves & River Phoenix cover). A Tartt feature is chronologically implausible: *The Secret History* was not published until September 1992, eleven months later. The marketplace Tartt attribution is most likely a vendor keyword artifact. Treated as unconfirmed, with no Tartt content.

- **The Modern Library: The 200 Best Novels in English Since 1950, by Carmen Callil & Colm Tóibín (Picador, 1999).** *The Secret History* is one of the 200 listed novels, with entry text by the editors. A multi-author signed copy exists, signed by 57 contributors at their respective entry pages, including Tartt at her entry — a distinct collecting category (signed at one's own catalogue entry) from inscribed personal copies or dedicated copies. Copy-in-hand confirmation of signature placement is outstanding.

- **Two marketplace listings (UK and AU)** could not be recovered, as the listings were removed and no archive snapshot exists; their item identities are unrecoverable.

## Source-Confidence Distribution (reference-wide)

A four-level confidence scheme classifies each claim by the strength of its support:

| Confidence label | Approx. count | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| VERIFIED | ~95 claims | Primary source directly confirms; citation URL active |
| CORROBORATED | ~140 claims | Two or more independent secondary sources agree |
| SINGLE SOURCE | ~80 claims | One credible source; no independent confirmation located |
| UNVERIFIED | ~25 claims | Collector tradition; no documentary source |

All corrections described above have been applied to the reference document.

## 2026 deep verification pass

A section-by-section re-check of the reference against the captured primary sources and public
records. Corrections applied to the reference as a result:

- **The Secret History, US first edition page count** corrected to **524 pp** (was 523), matching
  the contemporaneous *Newsweek* coverage, dealer collations, and the reference's own per-printing
  table.
- **"The Ambush" (§5.5):** removed the claim that the *Guardian Weekend* (June 25, 2005) printing
  was "the only known original magazine publication." The story also appeared in *Tin House*
  (Vol. 7 No. 2, 2006) and was reprinted in *The Best American Short Stories 2006*.
- **"Sleepytown" reprint (§5.1):** corrected the anthology to *Cowboys, Indians and Commuters: The
  Penguin Book of New American Voices*, ed. Jay McInerney (Penguin, **1994**) — previously given as
  "The Penguin Book of New American Voices (Penguin USA, 1997)."
- **Stephen King review (§16):** corrected the attributed wording. King's front-page NYTBR review
  (Oct 13, 2013) called the novel "a rarity … an extraordinary work of fiction"; the previously
  quoted "a triumph of storytelling" was not his wording.
- **James Wood LRB review (fn102):** removed a malformed/uncertain archive URL for the 1992 LRB
  review; the citation (LRB, Nov 19 1992) is retained.
- **Portis tribute word count (§6):** removed a conflicting second word-count for the same June 9,
  2020 NYTBR piece.
- **Goldfinch deluxe ISBN (§4.5A):** added a caveat that ISBN 9781408705100 is not a reliable
  edition discriminator (Open Library resolves it to the standard 2013 UK hardcover; it also
  appears in dealer records for later Deluxe printings — see §4.11).
- **Excerpt links:** the Rimbaud translation (§7.22) and the *Legendary Authors' Clothes* chapter
  (§7.25) now link their archived excerpt files; the *Guardian* "How Mattie Got Her Man" scans are
  linked in §7.1.

Claims confirmed against sources include: the $450,000 advance and 75,000 first printing, the
Sept 16 1992 publication date and core ISBNs, the 2014 Pulitzer and the awards/years in §16, the
Dutch *Het puttertje* true-first precedence, the Borzoi Reader Fall 1992 pre-publication excerpt,
the Heritage auction lot prices, the Ole Miss archive holdings, and the genuineness (and the
documented misattributions) of the §7 contributions.

**Items that remain single-sourced or unverified** (retained but flagged): the §1 genealogical
notes rest on one community bibliography project; several auction/association-copy figures and the
per-lot bidder counts come from aggregator listings rather than captured primary records; the
§8.1 *Vanity Fair* feature photographer attribution (Henry Benson vs. Dewey Nicks) is unsettled
