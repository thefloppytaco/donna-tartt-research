## Scope

This reference catalogues **Tier B** material: Tartt-related items where Tartt is the SUBJECT (interview, profile, cover blurb, signed poster) but is NOT the author of the contained text.

- Magazine issues containing Tartt interviews / profiles / reviews of her novels
- Newspaper supplements with Tartt feature coverage
- Signed posters / signed promotional ephemera bearing Tartt's signature on someone else's text
- Cover-blurb mentions of Tartt in TIME 100 / Pulitzer rosters and similar lists

Items fully verified as Tartt-AUTHORED (introductions, essays, fiction reprints, Q&A reprints) belong in `Donna_Tartt_Collectors_Reference.md` — primarily §5–§7. Cross-references are noted inline below where applicable.

Cover images for items in this file are stored under `assets/images/about/`.

## Format

Each entry uses the same block structure as the main reference for consistency: cover ref, bibliographic detail, Tartt-content note, and (if applicable) cross-reference to the main reference document.

## B.8 R.B. Russell, *Past Lives of Old Books and Other Essays* (Tartarus Press, 2020) — contains essay "Fragile Ivory Towers: The Critics and Donna Tartt"

**Cover:** `assets/images/about/EPH-PastLivesOldBooks-Russell-Tartarus-2020_cover_publisher.jpg`

**Item:** R.B. Russell's collected essays from Tartarus Press, an English fine-press publisher. Russell is editor of *Wormwood* magazine and a longtime literary essayist focused on supernatural fiction, decadence, and book collecting.

**Tartt content:** A standalone critical essay titled **"Fragile Ivory Towers: The Critics and Donna Tartt"** is one of the 40+ essays in the volume — listed in the publisher's table of contents alongside essays on Arthur Machen, Robert Aickman, Alain-Fournier, Pierre Louÿs, Denton Welch, J.M. Barrie, Quentin Crisp, and others. This is a substantive critical engagement with Tartt's work, not a passing reference. Length, exact subject (e.g., which novel or which critical reception episode), and full text not independently confirmed.

Relevant to a Tartt critical-reception subset (a thinly-documented niche in the main reference). Whether Tartarus Press issued earlier (hardcover or limited) editions of this title is not independently confirmed; the paperback is the current trade form.

## B.9 *Books to Die For: The World's Greatest Mystery Writers on the World's Greatest Mystery Novels*, ed. John Connolly & Declan Burke (Emily Bestler Books / Atria / Simon & Schuster, 2012) — Tana French's essay on Tartt's *The Secret History*

**Cover:** *(not yet available)*

**Item:** A 537-page anthology of 119 essays by mystery writers from 20 countries, each writing about a favorite mystery novel. Hardcover first edition published by Emily Bestler Books / Atria (Simon & Schuster imprint), October 2, 2012, ISBN 9781451696578 (HC) / 9781451696585 (PB/ebook). UK edition: Hachette UK, ISBN 9781444756517.

**Tartt content:** **Tana French wrote an essay championing Donna Tartt's *The Secret History*** as one of the great mystery novels. Per French's quoted essay text: *"For me, this book redefined the territory that mysteries can claim. When I started writing, more than ten years after I first read it, I was writing within a landscape that The Secret History had redrawn for me. I aim to write mysteries that take genre conventions as springboards, not as laws, and never as limitations on quality or scope: books where the real murder mystery isn't whodunit, but whydunit and what it means."* An aggregated description further frames French's piece as "the persuasive piece on the importance of Donna Tartt's *The Secret History* as the first book to use friendship circles as a subject for crime."

**Important attribution note:** Several dealer-side data feeds (notably an AbeBooks listing, ISBN 9781451696578) include "Donna Tartt" in their contributor-name autolist. This is **misleading**: Tartt did NOT write an essay for *Books to Die For*. She is the SUBJECT of Tana French's essay (which discusses TSH). The dealer-side conflation of "subjects of essays" with "contributing authors" is a common data-aggregator error and should not be treated as evidence of a Tartt-authored contribution. A signed-by-26-contributors copy of the first edition exists; Tartt is NOT among the 26 signers.

**References:**
- Simon & Schuster product page: https://www.simonandschuster.com/books/Books-to-Die-For/John-Connolly/9781451696585
- Goodreads: https://www.goodreads.com/en/book/show/15743453-books-to-die-for
- Hachette UK edition: https://www.hachette.co.uk/titles/john-connolly-2/books-to-die-for/9781444756517/

**Cross-reference / collector note:** Tana French wrote *In the Woods* (2007) and the Dublin Murder Squad series; she is part of the post-TSH wave of literary-crime writers who explicitly cite Tartt as foundational. French's essay is the highest-profile critical engagement with TSH from another working novelist in the published record. Useful adjacent material to the main reference's §3181 "Critical reception" area and the §16 "Awards and critical reception" section, and to a Tartt-related criticism-by-other-novelists subset.

The volume is also notable for the breadth of mystery-genre coverage: 100+ essays, signed copies (one listing carries 26 contributor signatures), with contributors including Michael Connelly, Patricia Cornwell, Kathy Reichs, Ian Rankin, James Lee Burke, Sara Paretsky.

## B.11 *The Modern Library: The 200 Best Novels in English Since 1950* by Carmen Callil and Colm Tóibín (Picador, London, 1999) — Tartt's *The Secret History* is in the canonical list; one documented multi-author signed copy bears Tartt's signature at her own entry

**Cover:** *(not yet available)*

**Item:** A reference work / canon-list by **Carmen Callil** (founder of Virago Press) and **Colm Tóibín** (Booker-shortlisted novelist) selecting 200 best English-language novels published 1950–1999. First UK edition, Picador, London, 1999. Hardcover with dust jacket. Donna Tartt's *The Secret History* (1992) is one of the 200 listed novels.

**Tartt content:** Two layers of significance:

1. **As subject (Tier B base):** Callil & Tóibín's entry for *The Secret History* in the 200-list constitutes one of the earliest book-form canonical placements of Tartt's debut novel — only seven years after publication (1992 → 1999). The entry text by Callil/Tóibín is critical-evaluation prose about TSH and would be of interest to anyone tracking critical reception of TSH.

2. **As signer of a documented unique copy (Tier-A overlap):** A specific signed copy held by **Ashton Rare Books** (Leicestershire, UK) is signed by **both Callil and Tóibín plus 57 of the contributing/listed authors** — each signing at the page of their own entry. **Donna Tartt is among those 57 author-signers**, having signed at her TSH entry. Other signatories on the same copy include Martin Amis, Margaret Atwood, Ian McEwan, Bret Easton Ellis, Salman Rushdie, Kazuo Ishiguro, plus several Nobel laureates. This makes the Ashton copy a multi-signed Tartt-bearing artifact.

**Bibliographic record:**

| Field | Data |
|---|---|
| Title | *The Modern Library: The 200 Best Novels in English Since 1950* |
| Authors | **Carmen Callil & Colm Tóibín** |
| Publisher | Picador, London (UK first edition) — also Atlantic Monthly Press (US) |
| Publication date | 1999 |
| Format | Hardcover with dust jacket |
| Status | Tartt's TSH is one of 200 listed novels |

**Reference:**
- **Ashton Rare Books, Leicestershire, UK** — listed on Biblio.com (https://www.biblio.com/details.php?dcx=1551305587) for the multi-signed copy. Per the dealer description, the copy carries 59 total signatures (the two authors + 57 contributors signing at their respective entries).

**Cross-reference / collector note:** This is a documented Tartt-signed reference-work copy in the same family as the Lenox Hill chapbook program (§5.2 / §8.3 / §10.7) and the Folio Society 500-copy signed limited (§7.14 / CON-011a). Should be cross-referenced when §10.9 (signed contributed-piece editions) is next revised — though the framing differs: in §10.9 Tartt is the contributing author, here she is the subject of someone else's entry but added her signature to that entry on a specific copy.

This is also one of the earliest documented Tartt signatures on a non-Tartt-authored book (signed-at-her-entry signing programs are an emerging Tartt-collecting category, distinct from inscribed-personal-copy and dedicated-to-someone signing).

## Items checked and not included

For provenance, the following items were checked and determined to have NO Tartt content (or to be chronologically implausible / unverifiable):

**Andy Warhol's Interview Magazine, Vol XXII No 11** — confirmed to be the **November 1991 issue** (Keanu Reeves & River Phoenix cover, *My Own Private Idaho* promotion, photographed by Paige Powell; contents include River Phoenix & Reeves 7pp, Ray Charles 4pp, Julie Delpy 6pp, Joe Mantegna 4pp, Peter Lindbergh photo essay). **Tartt is implausible:** *The Secret History* was not published until September 1992 — eleven months after this Nov 1991 issue. The comprehensive Donna Tartt interview archive at languageisavirus.com lists no *Interview* magazine appearance among Tartt's 1992 promotional cluster (Newsweek Sept 7, 1992 named six magazines featuring Tartt that month: *Vanity Fair*, *M*, *Esquire*, *Vogue*, *Elle*, *Mirabella* — *Interview* was NOT in that list). The listing's Tartt attribution is most likely either (a) a vendor keyword-search artifact, or (b) a passing mention. No confirmed Tartt content.

**ACTUEL N°31-32, July-August 1993, "SEXE" issue** — ✓✓ **CONFIRMED — POSITIVE FINDING. PRIOR NEGATIVE FINDING OVERTURNED.** Tartt **IS** in this issue: a full-page PORTRAITS feature, **"TARTT, AU POIL !" by Frédéric Taddeï, pp. 170–171**, with a John Foley / Olympe author portrait. Primary-source scans of both pages and a transcript are archived at `assets/sources/press_primary/ACTUEL-N31-32-1993-07_p170_Taddei-TARTT-AU-POIL_scan1of2.jpg`, `…_p170-171_…_scan2of2.jpg`, and `…_p170-171_Taddei-TARTT-AU-POIL_transcript.txt`. The piece is a polemical France-vs-USA essay ("Lire un aussi bon roman américain rend méchant") praising *The Secret History* / *Le Maître des illusions* (Plon) and using Tartt to critique the French literary scene. **This belongs with confirmed Tartt press, not in this section.**

> **Why the earlier "negative finding" was wrong (now superseded):** The prior resolution relied on photographs of the printed two-page TOC ("SOMMAIRE — UN NUMÉRO DOUBLE QUI PENSE À TOUT"), from which it was concluded that no Tartt content existed. The TOC photo did **not** capture the Portraits section (p. 170–171), so the feature was missed. The old TOC transcript at `assets/sources/press_primary/ACTUEL-N31-32-1993-07_TOC-transcript_NEGATIVE-FINDING.txt` should be re-labelled as an incomplete TOC, not a clearance. **Lesson:** a supplied TOC photo is not a complete index — a "byline/subject not in TOC" review cannot clear an issue when the TOC image is partial. The original plausibility hypothesis (French TSH launch period via Plon, 1993) was correct after all. *(Note: the p. 171 continuation scan curls into the binding gutter; a flat scan of p. 171 would let the transcript be completed verbatim.)*
